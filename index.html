Serafín Frache

Assistant Professor

Universidad de Montevideo



I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Economics at Universidad de Montevideo. My primary fields of interest are macroeconomics, numerical methods and applied econometrics. I have research experience in monetary economics, household income dynamics and their consumption and saving decisions, and how firms form their expectations.

Publications

  • “Buffer-Stock Saving and Households' Response to Income Shocks,” with Giulio Fella and Winfried Koeniger, International Economic Review, forthcoming.

Working Papers

  • “New information and inflation expectations among firms”, BIS Working Papers 781, Bank for International Settlements, April 2019, with Rodrigo Lluberas.
  • “Countercyclical capital buffers, dynamic provisions or both?” New Challenges in Central Banking: Monetary Policy Governance and Macroprudential Issues, SUERF - The European Money and Finance Forum, Cap. 9, November 2017, with Javier García-Cicco and Jorge Ponce.
  • “Countercyclical prudential tools in an estimated DSGE model”, Documentos de trabajo 2017001, Banco Central del Uruguay, 2017, with Javier García-Cicco and Jorge Ponce.
  • “Estimación de los efectos de la Ley de Inclusión Financiera en un marco Dinámico Estocástico de Equilibrio General”, Documentos de trabajo 2016006, Banco Central del Uruguay, 2016, with Juan Odriozola.
  • “Un modelo estocástico de equilibrio general para la economía uruguaya”, Documentos de trabajo 2016002, Banco Central del Uruguay, 2016.
  • “Estimating a Risky Term Structure of Uruguayan Sovereign Bonds”, Documentos de Trabajo (working papers) 0304, Department of Economics - dECON, 2004, with Gabriel Katz.

Work in Progress
